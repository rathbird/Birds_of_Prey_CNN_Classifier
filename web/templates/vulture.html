Vultures - What do we call them?

The only raptors which pal around with their peers are the odd looking vultures and condors. We've all heard of a murder of crows, but what would we call a group of vultures? Seems like such an easy question, but not so fast! It depends on what they are doing, and where they are doing it. If they are feeding on a carcass, they are a wake. Like all birds, you might call them a flock, if they are in flight, they are a kettle. They can also be referred to as a venue, volt, or committee. Clear as day, right?

Vultures have the hooked beak and large talons of other raptors, but they are more scavengers than hunters. They also don't have the grip strength to carry off their prey, which is why you so often find them at a wake, feasting on a carcass en masse. The largest flying bird is a vulture, the Andean Condor has a wingspan as long as a compact car (9-10ft), and can weigh more than 25lbs, twice as hefty as the big eagles.
