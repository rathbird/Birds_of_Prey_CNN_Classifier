Eagles - the Ultimate Raptor

Eagles are an apex predator having no natural enemies. Along with some vultures, eagles are the largest raptors, weighing up to 15 lbs (Steller, Philippine, Harpy) and having seven-foot wingspans (White-tailed, Steller). Other than size, noticeable characteristics are long, feather legs, large hooked beaks, and long strong talons. The bald eagle is has the heaviest verified load, carrying away a mule dear fawn weighing 15 lbs. Eagles will attack larger animals that they cannot carry, eating it at the kill site or tearing off pieces to carry back to the nest. Eagles have been known to bring down prey weighing over 80 lbs.

If you want to see an eagle up close, you might need to climb a mountain. Eagles build their nests in very tall trees or high cliffs. Eagles are also territorial, if you see two eagles in the same area, you are probably looking at a mated pair. Eagles typically mate for life, and usually lay two eggs per breeding cycle. Only one of the hatchlings survives, as the first one out of the egg will kill the second hatchling. Oddly enough, the parents do nothing to stop the fratricide. If the eggs are of a different gender, the female usually survives as she is bigger than the male.
